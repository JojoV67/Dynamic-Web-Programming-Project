Dr. Lisa Su

Chair and Chief Executive Officer

Lisa Su


Dr. Lisa Su is the Chair and CEO of AMD (Advanced Micro Devices), a leading semiconductor and computer processor manufacturer. Under her leadership, AMD has become a leader in high-performance computing and one of the fastest-growing semiconductor companies globally. She has been instrumental in integrating AMD's business units, sales, global operations, and infrastructure teams into a cohesive organization responsible for all aspects of product strategy and execution.

Education

Experience

Lisa Tzwu-Fang Su, born in November 1969 in Tainan, Taiwan, immigrated to the United States at age 3. Raised in a family that emphasized math and science, she developed an early interest in engineering. Su attended the Bronx High School of Science in New York City and then pursued electrical engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

During her undergraduate years, she worked on semiconductor research projects and interned at Analog Devices, sparking her interest in the field. She earned her bachelor's and master's degrees from MIT, followed by a PhD in electrical engineering, focusing on silicon-on-insulator (SOI) technology to enhance transistor efficiency. Su's thesis was titled "Extreme-submicrometer silicon-on-insulator (SOI) MOSFETs."

    Beginning her professional journey at IBM, Su held various technical and business roles over 13 years, contributing significantly to cutting-edge technologies. In 2012, she joined Advanced Micro Devices (AMD), quickly rising to become President and CEO in 2014. Su's leadership at AMD led to a remarkable resurgence, marked by innovative strategies and product development, notably the Ryzen processors.

    Su has served on the boards of Analog Devices, Cisco Systems, Inc., the Global Semiconductor Alliance, and the U.S. Semiconductor Industry Association. As of 2016 she has published over forty technical articles and coauthored a book chapter discussing next-generation consumer electronics.

    Dr. Su emphasizes innovation, collaboration, and inclusivity, advocating for diversity in the tech industry and promoting STEM education. Her achievements have earned her numerous accolades, including recognition as one of Fortune's "Most Powerful Women" and Forbes' "World's 100 Most Powerful Women." Su's career serves as an inspiration, highlighting the transformative power of vision and perseverance in the fast-paced world of technology.

Texas Instruments and IBM R&D

1994-1999

Lisa Su started at Texas Instruments, then joined IBM, quickly rising to VP of semiconductor R&D. There, she revolutionized the industry by integrating copper with semiconductor chips, boosting performance and setting new standards.

IBM Emerging Products Division

2000-2007

At IBM, Lisa Su held key leadership roles, including technical assistant to the CEO and director of emerging projects. She drove innovations like biochips and low-power semiconductors, boosting handheld device battery life and co-creating the Cell microprocessor. These achievements earned her a spot as one of MIT Technology Review's "Top Innovators Under 35" in 2001.

Freescale Semiconductor

2007-2011

Lisa Su joined Freescale Semiconductor in June 2007 as Chief Technology Officer, later becoming Senior Vice President and General Manager of the networking and multimedia group, credited with enhancing operations and leading the company to file for an IPO in 2011.

AMD Appointments

2012-2014

In January 2012, Lisa Su joined AMD as senior vice president and general manager, overseeing global business units and diversifying the company's market beyond PCs, culminating in her appointment as president and CEO on October 8, 2014, where she focused on strategic technology investments and streamlining operations to accelerate the development of new technologies.

AMD Diversification

2015-2016

During Lisa Su's time at AMD, she boosted non-PC product sales from 10% to 40% by February 2015. She shifted the focus to high-performance computing and graphics, developing FinFET-based chips. Her strategy paid off, with AMD's share value surging in July 2016, driven by successful turnaround plans and strategic deals like licensing server chip designs in China.

Ryzen

2017-Present

Lisa Su's leadership at AMD led to a surge in CPU market share following the launch of Zen chips in 2017, with Ryzen CPUs receiving positive reviews for their high thread counts and competitive pricing, culminating in Su becoming the first woman to top The Associated Press' annual CEO compensation survey in 2019, and later assuming the role of Chair after overseeing the completion of a reported $49 billion acquisition of Xilinx in February 2022.
